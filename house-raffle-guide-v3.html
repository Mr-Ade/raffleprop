Regulatory Overview

The Three-Layer Legal Framework

🚨 This must be read before anything else Running this business without approvals in place before any public advertising — including social media teasers, influencer briefings, or website launches — is a criminal offence under Nigerian law. The FCCPC can impose fines of ₦10,000,000 plus the full assessed promotion fee for non-registration. The LSLGA adds up to ₦1,000,000 or 2 years imprisonment. Do not post anything publicly until all approvals are confirmed in writing.

⚖Layer 1 — FCCPC (Federal)

The Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission governs all sales promotions nationwide under the FCCPA 2018 and the Sales Promotion Regulations 2005. This is the supreme authority. No campaign can run without their written approval.

Apply ≥ 21 days before any advertising begins

🏠Layer 2 — LSLGA (Lagos State)

If your property is in Lagos or your primary market is Lagos, the Lagos State Lotteries & Gaming Authority requires a separate licence. The LSLGA and FCCPC are not substitutes — you need both simultaneously.

Licence fee: ₦500,000 + ₦50,000 application

📜Layer 3 — FCCPA 2018 (Conduct)

Beyond approvals, the Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Act 2018 directly regulates every representation you make, every term in your ticket purchase contract, and how you treat every buyer throughout the campaign.

Applies continuously — not just at application stage

⚠ The Good Causes Fund — Your Most Critical Financial Question The LSLGA law requires that a portion of funds generated from promotions in Lagos be allocated to state causes: 20% infrastructure, 20% education, 20% environment, 20% social/health. Before setting any pricing or financial targets, obtain a formal written legal opinion on whether this applies to your gross ticket revenue, net revenue, or net profit. This question must be resolved before a single ticket is priced. If it applies to gross revenue on a ₦62.5M campaign, the financial model changes fundamentally.

Phase 00 — Before Legal Applications

Pre-Application Groundwork

These steps must be completed before you can even submit regulatory applications. They are the foundation everything else rests on.

A

Incorporate a Limited Liability Company with the CAC FCCPC Guidelines §4(a)

The FCCPC application form (Form CPC A) requires proof of CAC incorporation as the very first document. A business name registration alone is insufficient for a business of this nature and scale. Register a Limited Liability Company. Use a company name that reflects the platform’s long-term commercial identity — this entity will run all future campaigns, so name it accordingly. Cost: ₦30,000–₦100,000. Timeline: 1–2 weeks. After incorporation, open a dedicated corporate bank account. Never receive ticket payments into a personal account.

B

Obtain a Written Legal Opinion on the LSLGA Good Causes Fund LSLGA Guidelines §Good Causes

Before setting your financial model, ticket price, or minimum threshold, commission a formal written legal opinion from your property lawyer on this specific question: does the LSLGA Good Causes Fund apply to gross ticket revenue, net revenue after prize cost, or net profit after all costs? This single question determines whether the business model is viable in Lagos. Do not proceed without a written answer on your file.

C

Obtain Independent Property Valuation FCCPA 2018 §123(1)(d)

Section 123(1)(d) of the FCCPA 2018 prohibits making any warranty or guarantee about goods or services unless based on an adequate and proper test, with the burden of proof on the promoter. Your stated property value in every advertisement is a legal representation. Commission a NIESV-registered estate valuer to produce a formal valuation report before any price is advertised. This report is simultaneously: your legal substantiation, your FCCPC application document, and your most powerful trust signal. Estimated cost: ₦50,000–₦200,000.

D

Verify and Clear Property Title FCCPC Guidelines §4(d)

The FCCPC requires an undertaking to run a free and transparent promotion, and evidence that the prize item is available and genuine before the promotion commences. Your property lawyer must verify the Certificate of Occupancy or equivalent title document, confirm there are no encumbrances, disputes, mortgages, or liens, and confirm the property is legally available for transfer. Do not submit a regulatory application until a clean title is confirmed in writing by your lawyer. Any undisclosed encumbrance discovered after launch could constitute a false representation under Section 125 of the FCCPA 2018, attracting liability for damages to all buyers.

Phase 01 — Regulatory Approvals

Obtaining All Required Approvals

🚨 Critical Sequence — Apply in This Order You must have written approval from the FCCPC before any public advertisement, social media activity, influencer briefing, or ticket sale. The promotion legally begins at the moment of first public communication — not the first ticket sale. A teaser post on Instagram without FCCPC approval is already a violation.
01

Submit FCCPC Application (Form CPC A) — Minimum 6 Weeks Before Planned Launch FCCPC Guidelines Part 3

Submit to the FCCPC at least 21 days before the promotion starts, but budget 6 weeks to allow for their 10 working-day review, any Request for Further Evidence (RFE), and resolution time. The FCCPC offers a 48-hour emergency premium service for an additional ₦500,000 — this should be a last resort, not a plan.

Documents required with your application:

  • CAC Certificate of Incorporation (mandatory — no exceptions)
  • Proof of the property’s existence and acquisition — title documents, C of O or equivalent
  • NIESV independent valuation report confirming property value
  • Evidence the property is available for inspection before the promotion commences
  • Detailed description of how the promotion works: ticket price, cap, how winner is selected
  • Sell-in-time (campaign duration — must not exceed 1 year)
  • Location(s) of the promotion
  • Samples of all proposed advertising materials: social media content, video scripts, flyer designs
  • Undertaking to conduct a free and transparent promotion exercise
  • Draw date(s), draw method, and how the draw will be conducted
  • Eligibility criteria and any age restrictions or exclusions
  • Whether multiple entries are permitted and how many (yes — your bundle model)
  • Winning odds per ticket (total tickets ÷ 1 winner = stated odds ratio)
  • How winners will be selected, announced, and contacted
  • Closing date for entries
  • Whether winners will be used in post-event publicity (and their consent mechanism)
  • Contact details of your designated company representative
  • Any other regulatory approvals obtained or pending (e.g. LSLGA)

The FCCPC will conduct an initial review within 3 working days and issue a decision within 7 working days of a complete application. They may issue a Request for Further Evidence — respond immediately as the clock restarts.

02

Pay FCCPC Assessed Fees After Receiving Notice to Pay FCCPC Schedule 3

Do not pay fees before receiving the Commission’s Notice of Decision (NOD) approving your application — the total promotion fee less the application fee is paid after approval. Fee structure:

03

FCCPC Approval — The Notice of Decision (NOD) FCCPC Guidelines Part 5

Once you receive the written NOD approving your promotion, it will include: your approval reference number, any conditions attached to the approval, and payment instructions for the total promotion fee. File this NOD safely — it is your legal authority to operate. Any change to the terms of your promotion after this point requires the FCCPC’s prior written approval before implementation. If you change ticket prices, extend the campaign, or alter the draw date without approval, this is a violation carrying up to ₦5,000,000 in penalties.

04

Apply to the LSLGA — Concurrent with FCCPC Application LSLGA Guidelines §Application

If your property is in Lagos or your primary audience is Lagos-based, apply to the Lagos State Lotteries and Gaming Authority simultaneously with your FCCPC application. The LSLGA process requires: 3 copies of competition rules, 3 copies of the prize structure, eligibility conditions, and proposed draw dates and intervals. The Board determines fees from submitted documents.

LSLGA Fees:

  • Application fee: ₦50,000
  • Licence fee: ₦500,000 (per campaign cycle)
  • Lottery levy: ₦25,000 per draw interval

The LSLGA Code of Conduct requires that all competitions give equally favourable chances to all participants. Your ticket system must ensure each ticket has an identical, mathematically equal chance of winning.

05

Structure the Competition Correctly — Not a Lottery LSLGA Guidelines §1 & FCCPC Act

Your lawyer must structure the promotion as a promotional competition, not a lottery. The mechanism that achieves this is a genuine skill element that forms part of the entry process. This cannot be a decorative checkbox — it must be a real, documented eligibility condition. The question must be answered correctly as a condition of valid entry (even if trivially easy). Your lawyer must draft this formally and it must appear on every entry form, ticket purchase screen, and in the FCCPC application. Document the skill element explicitly in your T&Cs and your regulatory submissions.

06

Embed Escrow Arrangement Before Launch FCCPC Guidelines §4(d) — Transparency Undertaking

Arrange escrow with a named Nigerian bank (GTBank, Zenith Bank, First Bank, or Stanbic IBTC) or a licensed escrow provider before any tickets go on sale. All ticket proceeds must flow into escrow, not your operational account. Funds are released to you only after draw completion and winner verification. This is simultaneously: your legal protection, your buyer protection, and your single most powerful trust signal. Refer to it prominently and specifically in all marketing — name the bank holding the funds.

Phase 03 — Legal Documents

Terms & Conditions — FCCPA-Compliant Drafting

📋 Sections 127–129, FCCPA 2018 — Prohibited Contract Terms Your T&Cs are a contract with every ticket buyer. The FCCPA 2018 prohibits contract terms that are unfair, unreasonable, or unjust. A term is unlawful if: it is excessively one-sided in your favour; any limitation of your liability was not drawn to the buyer’s attention conspicuously before purchase; or a buyer was not given adequate opportunity to comprehend the terms before payment. Buried terms or pre-ticked boxes that wave important rights are unenforceable and may constitute violations. Your lawyer must draft the T&Cs and they must be reviewed and approved as part of the FCCPC application.
1

Mandatory T&C Contents FCCPC Guidelines §4 & FCCPA 2018 §114

All documents presented to consumers must be in plain language under Section 114 of the FCCPA 2018 — language that an ordinary consumer with average literacy can understand without undue effort. Your T&Cs must include:

  • Full company name, CAC registration number, and registered address
  • FCCPC approval reference number and date of approval
  • LSLGA licence number (if applicable)
  • Full property description: address, size, title type, title reference number, NIESV valuation
  • Ticket price, total cap, and bundle pricing structure
  • Sell-in-time: campaign open date, campaign close date (not to exceed 12 months from FCCPC approval)
  • Draw date, time, location, and method — must match what was submitted to FCCPC
  • Minimum ticket threshold — and exact refund process if not met
  • Skill question — stated as a genuine eligibility condition for entry
  • Winning odds per ticket (expressed as a ratio e.g. 1 in 25,000)
  • Escrow bank name and confirmation that funds are held in escrow until draw
  • Winner verification requirements: ID, BVN, NIN, and timeline to respond
  • Property transfer process and timeline after winner verification
  • Refund policy — conspicuously drawn to buyer’s attention before payment
  • Tax obligations of the winner — clearly stated
  • How results will be announced and how winners will be contacted
  • Whether winners may be used in post-event publicity and the opt-out mechanism
  • Dispute resolution process and governing law (Federal Republic of Nigeria)
  • The statement: “This promotion has been approved by the Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission” — mandatory in all documents per FCCPC Guidelines
2

How T&Cs Must Be Presented at Purchase FCCPA 2018 §128

Section 128 of the FCCPA 2018 requires that any notice limiting your liability or imposing obligations on the buyer must be drawn to their attention “in a conspicuous manner and form that is likely to attract the attention of an ordinarily alert consumer.” Section 128(2) requires that the buyer is given adequate opportunity to receive and comprehend the terms before they are required to pay.

  • T&Cs must be displayed in full and be readable before the payment button is active — not a pop-up that disappears
  • The T&C acceptance checkbox must be un-ticked by default — pre-ticked boxes violate both FCCPA 2018 and NDPR
  • Any limitation of liability clause must appear in bold or otherwise visually prominent — not buried in small print
  • The refund policy must be specifically highlighted on the purchase screen, not only in the full T&C document
  • The FCCPC approval statement must appear on the purchase screen above the payment button
  • Your system must store the timestamp of T&C acceptance for every transaction as a permanent legal record
3

Any Change to Approved Terms Requires FCCPC Prior Approval FCCPC Guidelines §4(l)

Once the FCCPC has approved your promotion, any change to the approved terms — ticket price, closing date, draw date, prize description, minimum threshold, or any other material term — requires the FCCPC’s prior written approval before implementation. Making changes without approval carries a penalty of up to ₦5,000,000. Build a formal change-request process into your admin workflow. Your campaign management system must not allow changes to live campaign parameters without an admin confirmation that FCCPC approval has been obtained.

Phase 04 — Payment & Ticketing

Payment Systems & Transaction Records

📋 Section 118, FCCPA 2018 — Written Transaction Record Required for Every Purchase Section 118 of the FCCPA 2018 requires that a written record of every transaction be provided to the consumer. This record must include: your full company name, your registered business address, the date of the transaction, a description of goods/services (ticket number and campaign name), the unit price, quantity, and total price including any applicable taxes. Your ticket confirmation system is a legal document, not just a courtesy. Every email, SMS, and WhatsApp confirmation must contain all these elements.

💳Payment Gateways — Minimum Two

  • Paystack — Primary. Register at paystack.com/ng. Complete business KYC with CAC certificate. Accepts card, bank transfer, USSD. Server-side API integration only.
  • Flutterwave — Secondary/failover. Register at flutterwave.com. Handles international cards for diaspora buyers. Server-side API integration only.
  • Direct Bank Transfer — Backup. Publish corporate account number. Buyer references their name and phone. Confirm manually or via bank webhook.
  • Opay/Moniepoint/Kuda — Digital wallets for buyers without traditional bank accounts.

Never store card numbers or CVV codes. All card data is handled exclusively by the payment gateways. You store only gateway transaction references.

🎪Ticket Numbering & Confirmation

Every buyer must receive a unique ticket number plus a written confirmation containing all FCCPA Section 118 required fields. Set up three parallel confirmation channels:

  • Email: Automated via Mailchimp or Brevo. Must include: company name, address, date, ticket number(s), campaign name, unit price, quantity, total paid, FCCPC approval reference.
  • WhatsApp: Via WhatsApp Business API (Twilio, Vonage). Shorter summary with ticket number and draw date.
  • SMS: Via Termii or Africa’s Talking. Ticket number, campaign name, draw date, and FCCPC approval reference.

WhatsApp Marketing FCCPA 2018 §124 — No Pressure Tactics

Influencer Partnerships FCCPA 2018 §125–126 — Third-Party Representations

Under Section 125(2)(a) of the FCCPA 2018, a person acting on behalf of a supplier cannot falsely represent that they have any sponsorship, approval or affiliation that they do not have. All influencer content about your promotion is a legal representation made on your behalf. You are liable for representations made by influencers promoting your competition.

  • Provide every influencer with a written content brief stating: exact property value, FCCPC approval statement to include, prohibited words (“lottery”, “guaranteed win”, exaggerated value claims), and mandatory disclosures

Phase 07 — Draw Day

Conducting the Draw — Full Regulatory Compliance

🚨 FCCPC Must Be Notified at Least 14 Days Before Every Draw FCCPC Guidelines Part 8(a) — ₦100,000 Penalty for Late Notice Invitations to the FCCPC to monitor draws must be received not less than 14 days before the scheduled draw. This is a hard legal deadline. Build it into your calendar as a non-negotiable task at the end of Week 6.
1

Send Draw Invitation to FCCPC — 14+ Days in Advance FCCPC Guidelines Part 8(a)

Send a formal written invitation to the FCCPC stating: draw date, time, and venue; draw method (RNG + physical); full address; contact person and phone number on the day. Also notify the LSLGA with draw date and interval details. Keep written acknowledgement from both bodies on file. Having FCCPC representation at your draw is simultaneously a legal obligation and your most powerful trust signal — mention their presence in all draw-day marketing.

2

Draw Method — Use Both Digital and Physical LSLGA §7 — Equal Chances Required

  • Digital RNG: Use Random.org — publicly verifiable, internationally trusted. Screen-record the entire session. Screenshot the result with timestamp. This is your primary legal evidence.
  • Physical Draw: Print all ticket numbers, seal in a transparent drum, and have an independent witness (notary public, lawyer, or commissioner for oaths) draw a ticket on camera
  • Both methods must produce the same winning number to be valid — in practice, use RNG to produce the number, then have the physical draw locate that printed number from the drum to confirm it exists
  • The draw must comply with the LSLGA requirement that chances are equally favourable to all participants — your ticket numbering system must be sequential, non-duplicable, and cover the full range stated
3

Draw Day Logistics

  • Venue: the property itself (most credible) or a hotel event room
  • Present: your property lawyer, a notary public or commissioner for oaths, a bank representative from your escrow institution, FCCPC monitor, LSLGA monitor (if Lagos), at least 2 independent community witnesses
  • Professional video crew recording the full event start to finish
  • Simultaneous live streams on Facebook, YouTube, and Instagram — publicised 48 hours in advance
  • MC to explain each step of the draw process clearly on camera
  • Display the full locked ticket registry on screen for public verification
  • Draw 3 backup winning tickets (2nd and 3rd place) on the day — seal them and keep in admin records in case primary winner cannot be verified
4

Post-Draw: Submit Winner List to FCCPC Within 3 Days FCCPC Guidelines Part 8(b) — Hard Deadline

Within 3 days of the draw, submit to the FCCPC: the full list of winners (in this case, the 1 winner plus 2 backup winners) with their full names, phone numbers, detailed addresses, and prize details. This is not optional — it is a mandatory post-draw filing. Keep a copy of your submission on file.

5

Winner Verification & Property Transfer

  • Contact winner immediately via phone, WhatsApp, email, and SMS simultaneously
  • Winner has 48–72 hours to respond — state this in T&Cs and announce publicly at draw
  • Winner submits: government-issued ID, BVN for banking verification, NIN, and proof of ticket purchase
  • Your lawyer prepares the Deed of Assignment transferring property to winner
  • Both parties sign before a notary public
  • File for Governor’s Consent at State Land Bureau if required
  • Hand over all original title documents, keys, survey plan, and formal handover letter
  • Record and publish the entire handover on video — this is your most powerful future marketing asset
6

Submit End of Promotion Report Within 21 Days FCCPC Form CPC B — Mandatory Filing

Within 21 days of the promotion’s completion (the later of: draw date, or winner property transfer completion), file Form CPC B (End of Sales Promotion Report) with the FCCPC. This must include: number of draws conducted, draw dates, how draws were conducted, number of winners, categories of winners, whether all prizes have been redeemed, and contact details for all winners. This filing closes out the campaign from a regulatory perspective. File it on time — non-compliance attracts sanctions.

7

Minimum Threshold Not Met — Refund Process FCCPA 2018 §123 — Honoring Representations

If you stated in your T&Cs that a full refund will be issued if the minimum threshold is not reached, that promise is a legal representation under Section 123 of the FCCPA 2018 and must be honoured exactly. Failure to issue promised refunds would constitute a false, misleading, or deceptive representation and expose you to liability for damages to all buyers. Your refund system must be able to execute within the timeline stated in the T&Cs.

  • Paystack refund API: call for each transaction reference
  • Flutterwave refund API: call for each transaction reference
  • Bank transfers: manual refunds from corporate account with documentation
  • Notify every buyer via email, SMS, and WhatsApp on the day refunds are initiated

💬The Fundamental Principle

The FCCPA 2018 was enacted to eliminate “misleading, unfair, deceptive or unconscionable marketing, trading and business practices” (Section 17(g)). Every requirement in this guide — the approvals, the disclosed T&Cs, the NIESV valuation, the escrow, the FCCPC monitoring at draws — exists to protect your buyers.

Run this business with that principle as your foundation, not as an obstacle. A promoter who genuinely complies — who obtains approvals, publishes documents, holds funds in escrow, invites regulators to their draws, and transfers property to winners within the stated timeline — is a promoter whose buyers become advocates. In Nigeria’s market, that advocacy is more valuable than any advertising budget.

Compliance is not the cost of doing business. It is your competitive advantage.
